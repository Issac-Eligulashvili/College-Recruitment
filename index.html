OUR MISSION

The mission of the California Institute of Technology is to expand human knowledge and benefit society through research integrated with education. We investigate the most challenging, fundamental problems in science and technology in a singularly collegial, interdisciplinary atmosphere, while educating outstanding students to become creative members of society.

OUR VALUES

Honor

Caltech promotes a culture of respect and responsibility, encouraging students and faculty to uphold academic standards and personal accountability in all endeavors.

Integrity

The institute emphasizes ethical conduct in research and education, fostering an environment where honesty and transparency are foundational to academic and scientific pursuits.

Equity

Caltech is committed to creating an inclusive community that values diverse perspectives, ensuring equal access to opportunities and resources for all members of its community.

Student Activities!

Ditch Day

"Wake up, frosh, it's Ditch Day!"

A tradition with origins that stretch back to 1921, Ditch Day has evolved over the years but persists as one of Caltech's most beloved and zany events. Known for its "stacks," themed puzzle-solving adventures designed by graduating fourth years, Ditch Day involves logic challenges, high jinks across campus, and creative storytelling uniquely characteristic of Caltech.

Pi Day

Irrational Yet Entertaining

CCID invites faculty, staff, postdoctoral scholars, alumni, and students to celebrate Pi Day! Pi-zza will be served at noon, followed by pi-e and candy. Participants will get a chance to engage in fun games and activities. Those who are present at 1:59 PM will have a chance to win a grand pi-ze. There will be fun surprises for everyone! Take advantage of this unique opportunity while supplies last!

Halloween Pumpkin Drop

Every Halloween at midnight, Dabney House—one of Caltech's eight undergraduate student houses—stages a pumpkin drop from the top of Caltech Hall, the highest point on campus. At the event, begun in 1972 and now a Caltech tradition, students drop pumpkins frozen in liquid nitrogen from the top of Caltech Hall to see if they will give off a brief triboluminescent glow as they hit the ground and shatter.
